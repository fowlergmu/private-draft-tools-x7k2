Draft Strategy Acronyms

BPA — Best Player Available
Strategy of always drafting the highest-ranked player on the board, regardless of which positions you've already filled. Tends to win earlier rounds; conflicts with need-filling late.
ADP — Average Draft Position
The pick number at which a player is typically drafted across many drafts. Helpful for spotting "reaches" (drafting before ADP) and "values" (player still available past ADP).
VOB — Value Over Baseline
How much a player's projected points exceed the replacement-level player at the same position. Used as a within-position value signal — cross-position comparison is unreliable since each position has its own baseline. Closely related to VORP/VBD.
ECR — Expert Consensus Ranking
An aggregated ranking pooled from many fantasy experts (FantasyPros pioneered this format). Smoother than any single expert's list because it averages out idiosyncratic opinions.
VORP / VBD
Value Over Replacement Player / Value-Based Drafting. The general family of approaches VOB belongs to.
EV — Expected Value
Internal score this engine assigns to each player, computed as the average of adjusted floor and ceiling. The basis for VOB.

Roster Positions

QB — Quarterback
Usually 1 starter per team; 2 in superflex leagues.
RB — Running Back
Typically 2 starters. Premium scarce position in standard formats.
WR — Wide Receiver
Typically 2–3 starters. The deepest position in most leagues.
TE — Tight End
Usually 1 starter. Thin position — the top 3–5 tight ends have a big gap to the rest.
FLEX (FX)
A starter slot that accepts RB, WR, or TE. Sometimes "WR/RB" or "WR/RB/TE" depending on league rules.
DEF / DST / D/ST
Team Defense / Special Teams. You draft a whole team's defensive unit, not individual defenders. Three different abbreviations for the same thing.
BN (Bench)
Non-starting roster slots. Players here don't score but can be moved into starting slots week-to-week.

Scoring Formats

PPR — Points Per Reception
Format that awards 1 fantasy point per catch, on top of yardage and TD scoring. Boosts WR and pass-catching RB value.
Half PPR (0.5 PPR)
0.5 points per reception. The most common modern format. Sits between standard and full PPR in reception-heavy player value.
Standard (Non-PPR)
No bonus for receptions. Yardage and TDs only. Increasingly rare — favors workhorse RBs.
Superflex
Format with a second flex slot that also accepts QBs. Massively boosts QB value — you'll see QBs go in round 1.

Engine Concepts

Tier
A group of players considered roughly equivalent in expected value. Tier breaks reflect real talent gaps — you'd rather have any tier 2 RB than the best tier 3 RB.
Run Risk
Engine's estimate of the chance a positional "run" happens before your next pick — i.e. how likely the elite players at this position get drafted away. Computed from team needs around the snake and remaining elite supply.
Snake Draft
Draft order reverses each round. If you pick 3rd in round 1, you pick 10th in round 2 (in a 12-team league), then 3rd again in round 3. Equalizes value across draft positions.
Flex RB%
Assumed share of flex slots league-wide that get filled by RBs. Affects the VOB baseline calculation. Default 40 is reasonable for most leagues.
